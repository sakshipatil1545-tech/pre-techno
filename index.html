CAT III assessments for tunnel and station ventilation systems

CFD verification · fire safety · egress time · independent approval

Background

Fire safety is a complex discipline requiring a system‑based approach. Two principal effects are structural damage and smoke management. CFD is recognised as a valid tool for technical assessment of tunnel ventilation design.

PRE Technologies developed a methodology using strategically selected CFD simulations to independently verify safe design against fire scenarios, ensuring key criteria (egress time) are met.

Methodology

3D transient CFD

Navier-Stokes, finite volume, load case matrix. Turbulence model selected per flow nature.

Conservative HRR

Peak heat release rate sustained (no growth). Source/sink terms for smoke & heat.

Species transport

Stefan‑Maxwell diffusion, thermal diffusion. Radiation modelled (computationally heavy).

Back‑layering

Smoke reverse flow when ventilation insufficient – captured in transient simulations.

Fire dynamics in tunnels

Up to 4x HRR compared to open fire. Heat feedback much stronger in tunnels. Fire dynamics dominated by fuel & ventilation. For CAT III, peak HRR sustained, fire growth not modelled (conservative).
